Julian Assange

Julian Assange is the creator of Wikileaks, an international non-profit organisation that publishes news leaks and classified media provided by anonymous sources. Due to political persecution he has been in confinement since June 2012 while fighting extradition to the United States. Documents published by Wikileaks show evidence of war crimes, violations of international treaties, human rights abuses and corruption.

Julian created a platform for whistleblowers to shine a light on abuses of power. He has paid for this with his freedom, deteriorating health and separation from his loved ones.

Ross Ulbricht Ross Ulbricht created the first darknet market, the Silk Road, as an exercise in free market capitalism, a website where users were free to post and buy whatever they felt necessary so long as no party was being harmed. For creating the Silk Road, Ross was given 40 years and two life sentences in US prison without the possibility of parole. All of his charges were nonviolent and no victim was named at his trial. For comparison, similar crimes have received sentences of 64 months in prison. Ross was made an example to deter future criminals. His sentence does not fit the crime of an idealistic 25 year old.

Miscarriage of Justice

Both Julian Assange and Ross Ulbricht were early adopters of cryptocurrency and pioneers of the industry. They represent core values of our community, freedom of speech and free markets. Both are suffering ongoing political persecution. Political and human rights scholars have called their cases miscarriages of justice, warranting intervention and advocating for their freedom. Both created platforms to protect human rights, both are incarcerated because they brought freedom to others.

Why Freemoon?

Both Wikileaks and the Silk Road were early adopters of Bitcoin. They helped spread the adoption of Bitcoin and its use cases during its precarious early years. Because of this, the cryptocurrency community is forever indebted to these high-profile trail blazers and we here at Freemoon have found a way to pay back that debt. Inspired by cutting edge tokenomics, we have combined cryptocurrency’s final destination of the moon with the cause of freedom for its earliest advocates. The result is Freemoon. Freemoon’s protocol appeals to both the altruist and the hodler.

How Freemoon Works

Freemoon is a deflationary token, after each buy and sell transaction a 10% transaction fee is subtracted and will be distributed as follows:

5%

of the transaction will be split evenly into a single treasury address, and will be donated to the legal defenses of Julian Assange and Ross Ulbricht. Funds will be manually remitted periodically.

3%

of every transaction is automatically redistributed to existing token holders via reflection.

2%

of every transaction is put back into the liquidity pool and LP tokens are burned, creating a deflationary supply.

Forever Deflationary Supply/Burn

Freemoon is programmed to reward holders while increasing in both liquidity and value. We burned all of the developer and founder tokens after launch, 20% of the total supply, and sent it to a black hole address; as the address also participates in the protocol, it accumulates more tokens, thereby effectively removing them from circulation. There is no limit to the burn, the black hole will keep growing, increasing the scarcity of Freemoon.

Liquidity

Having tokens being burnt from both buyers and sellers are what give deflationary tokens a special advantage over the tokens of yore. Doing so stabilizes the price relatively early, meaning there is a buffer against large sell offs of the coin.

Token Lock

For further price stability, Freemoon will use DxLock to restrict 50% of the supply for a duration of 4 years.
